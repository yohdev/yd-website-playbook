# 05 — Target Audience
**Client:** Daly Orthodontics
**Owner:** sales
**Last updated:** 2026-04-24
**Source:** Otter discovery notes — Victor Daly × Andrew Hewitt, 2026-04-21

---

## Primary Audience

**Who:** Prospective orthodontic patients in Burlington, North Carolina and the surrounding area — adults seeking treatment for themselves, and parents researching care on behalf of their children. Likely arriving via Google Search or Google Ads. Not highly technical; decision is personal and often emotionally driven (appearance, confidence, cost, convenience).

**Job they're hiring for:** Quickly confirming that Daly Orthodontics offers the specific orthodontic service they need (braces, aligners, etc.), then finding the fastest path to contact or book a consultation.

**How they decide:**

- They scan the homepage and services pages first — if it reads "orthodontist" clearly and immediately, they stay. If it looks like a general dental site, they leave.
- Proximity and Google presence matter: they're searching locally and likely already comparing two or three practices from the search results page.
- Trust signals close the deal — photos of the practice, the doctor, and real patient outcomes build enough confidence to make contact.
- Ease of next step: a visible phone number, a contact form, or an online booking link needs to be within one click of wherever they land.

**What makes them bounce:**

- Landing on content that doesn't match their intent — blog posts about oral surgery, general dentistry, or anything that signals this is not an orthodontic practice. This has already been documented as a live problem; Pearl Sites SEO content has driven wrong-specialty phone calls and forced the client to add negative keywords to Google Ads.
- A cluttered or confusing mobile experience — mobile nav is currently identified as crowded and poorly designed, and the majority of local search traffic arrives on mobile.
- No clear indication of location — patients need to quickly confirm the practice serves Burlington, NC.

**Where they find us:**

- Google Search (organic SEO — currently managed, poorly, by Pearl Sites)
- Google Ads (active campaign, managed separately)
- Likely some word-of-mouth and social media referrals driven by Maris Sooul's social media management

---

## Secondary Audience

**Who:** Parents in the Burlington, NC area researching orthodontic care for their children — teens primarily, though some younger patients depending on the practice's case mix.

**What they need:** Reassurance that the practice is qualified, child-friendly, and easy to deal with logistically (location, hours, insurance). They may spend more time reading and comparing than adult patients seeking treatment for themselves. They are also more likely to be the ones filling out a contact form or calling to ask questions before committing to a consultation.

---

## Anti-Audience

> Who we are NOT designing for.

General dental patients, oral surgery patients, and anyone seeking non-orthodontic dental services. This is not a hypothetical concern — misaligned traffic is already a documented, active problem caused by Pearl Sites publishing generic dental SEO content across its client base. That content has driven irrelevant phone calls and wasted ad spend. Every design and content decision in Milestone 1 should reinforce a single message: this is an orthodontics practice, not a dental office. The site must never be ambiguous on this point.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

1. Does the site make it immediately clear — within the first scroll — that Daly Orthodontics is an orthodontic practice, not a general dentist?
2. Can a prospective patient on a mobile device find a contact method or booking path in under ten seconds?
3. Does the site avoid any content, framing, or imagery that could attract non-orthodontic dental traffic?
4. Do the services listed match what a local patient searching "orthodontist Burlington NC" would expect to find?
5. Does the site build enough trust (doctor photo, practice photos, service descriptions) to move a first-time visitor toward making contact?

---

## Key Insight from Call

> "They published a blog post that basically told people not to go to an orthodontist — on our orthodontist's website. We've had to add negative keywords in Google Ads just to stop getting calls from dental patients."

This is the sharpest signal from the discovery call. The audience problem at Daly Orthodontics is not about finding more people — it is about stopping the wrong people from arriving while making it unmistakably easy for the right people to convert. The Milestone 1 migration must eliminate all misaligned content and establish a clear, specialty-specific identity from the first page load.

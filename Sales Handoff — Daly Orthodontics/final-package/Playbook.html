Section 01

The overview.

In one paragraph: we lift Daly Orthodontics off ProSites and onto a WordPress site you fully own before your June 2026 billing cutoff. Then we rebuild the brand expression around the logo and colors you already like — new typography, new voice, new imagery, new UX — without touching the mark itself. Two projects. Two timelines. One team, one playbook.

"I just want to own my own stuff. I don't want to have to go through someone every time I want to change something on my own website." — Victor, Discovery Call, 2026-04-21

That sentence is the whole reason this project exists. The rest of this document is how we deliver on it.

Section 02

Why we're doing this.

The current site has two real problems and one perceived one. Naming them honestly here means we can scope against them directly.

Problem 1 — You don't own your website.

ProSites owns the platform, the content management, the page structure, and — most importantly — your ability to make changes without paying for them. Every time you need a new page, you pay. Every time the nav needs a tweak, you pay. That is not a vendor relationship; that is a tollbooth.

Problem 2 — The blog is working against you.

ProSites publishes generic dental content on your site to boost their SEO metrics across their client base. For a general-dental practice, that's mostly harmless. For an orthodontics-only specialty practice, it's actively hostile — it attracts the wrong patients, wastes ad spend, and (in at least one published post) has literally told readers not to visit an orthodontist. This is the harder problem, and it's invisible until you see the Google Ads negative-keyword list you've had to build.

The perceived problem — "it looks dated."

It doesn't, exactly. Your logo is strong. Your palette (deep green, warm gold) is strong. What's dated is the expression of the brand — the stock hero photo, the mustard-yellow accents that don't match the logo's gold, the crowded mobile nav, the four competing scheduling widgets. The brand has never been allowed to speak in its own voice. Milestone 2 is where we fix that.

In other words

The logo Daly Orthodontics commissioned is a premium, considered, serious piece of design. The site wrapped around it is a template. Milestone 1 gets you off the template. Milestone 2 lets the logo finally breathe.

Section 03

Two milestones, two bets.

We separate these deliberately. M1 is the forced move — the deadline is real. M2 is the optional move — the upside is real. You can sign M1 without committing to M2. You can sign them together. What you can't do is combine them, because the timeline won't permit it.

Milestone 1 — Lift & shift

Goal
Site lives on WordPress, under your ownership, before June 2026.
Scope
One-for-one migration of existing pages, content, and URLs. Blog content triage. Mobile nav cleanup. Native WordPress blocks — no third-party page builders.
Not in scope
New branding, new copy, new photography, new visual design.
Timeline
~6 weeks. Must complete before the June ProSites billing date.
The bet
You get ownership, a clean exit, and no SEO damage. The site still looks like the site looks today — just rebuilt on your infrastructure.

Milestone 2 — Brand-forward redesign

Goal
The site finally looks and reads like the logo promises.
Scope
New typography system (Fraunces + Inter). New voice. New homepage structure. New photography direction. Style guide and WordPress theme.json. Everything in this document, in other words.
Kept
Logo primary mark. Deep green + warm gold palette. URL structure (SEO equity preserved from M1).
Timeline
~8 weeks, scheduled after M1 go-live. No forced deadline.
The bet
The brand expression catches up with the mark. First-time visitors can tell in five seconds that this is an orthodontics specialist in Burlington — and they want to book.
On paying twice

You raised this on the discovery call, and it's a fair concern. The answer: M1 is the price of ownership, not the price of delay. You would pay for this work whether M2 existed or not. M2 is additive, and it builds directly on M1's foundation — the URLs, content, hosting, and CMS setup all carry forward unchanged. There is no rework between the two.

Section 04

The six phases.

Every YohDev project runs through the same six phases. Each one has a tangible deliverable and an explicit approval gate before we move to the next. You never wonder what we're doing or why.

01

Intake

Read the handoff, ask the canonical questions, document decisions. You approve the intake summary before we spend any time building.

02

Research

Crawl the current site, audit the blog, screenshot the competition. You see what's there before we decide what to keep.

03

System

Lock design tokens — color, type, spacing. This is where the new voice starts to feel real.

04

Homepage

Build one page to high fidelity. The bet is that if the homepage lands, the rest of the site has a clear template to follow.

05

Style guide

Document the components so your site can grow beyond the homepage without you calling us every time.

06

Package

Assemble the final deliverable, write the deployment README, archive the source handoff. You can walk away with the whole thing in a folder.

A note on approvals

We don't move to the next phase without you signing off on the current one. That's not a formality — it's how we keep scope, timeline, and budget honest. If something changes direction at Phase 3, the cost of fixing it there is a fraction of what it would be at Phase 5.

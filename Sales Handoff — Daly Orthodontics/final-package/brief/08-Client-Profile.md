# Client Profile — Victor Daniel Daly (Daly Orthodontics)

> Read before the first working session.

---

## Communication style

- **Preferred channel:** Email — victordaniel16@gmail.com is the primary contact. Calls confirmed for structured check-ins (discovery call was comfortable and conversational). No Slack or Loom preference established yet.
- **Response cadence:** Unknown — ask in Phase 01, but he responded quickly enough to schedule and attend a discovery call, which suggests reasonable availability.
- **Meeting style:** Camera-on, comfortable with screen shares. He engaged well on the live discovery call and Andrew used screen share to walk through examples on the existing site. Expect this pattern to continue for milestone reviews.

---

## Decision-making

- **Who signs off:** Victor Daniel Daly (business side) and his wife (the practicing orthodontist — name not captured). Both are co-owners. Expect joint sign-off on anything that touches the brand or the look of the site.
- **Who else is in the room:** Wife is the silent second decision-maker. She wasn't on the discovery call but she will be reviewing portfolio examples with Victor before the Friday follow-up. Factor her into every presentation.
- **How fast they decide:** Fast when motivated by a deadline. The June billing cutoff is a genuine forcing function — he wants to be out of ProSites before that invoice hits. Expect same-call or 24-hour decisions on Milestone 1 scope and pricing if the quote lands cleanly.
- **What tips them → yes:** Concrete timelines with a clear path to the June deadline, full ownership language (he used the word "ownership" repeatedly), and examples he can show his wife. Specificity wins here — he pushed back on vagueness during the call and wanted to understand exactly what migration would look like.
- **What tips them → no:** Anything that sounds like a new vendor dependency. He is coming from a vendor relationship where he couldn't control his own site and every change cost extra — recreating that dynamic, even accidentally, will kill the deal. Avoid open-ended retainers or language that implies ongoing gatekeeping.

---

## What they care about most

1. Getting out of ProSites before June without paying another month — this is the number one priority and the reason he booked the call.
2. Owning everything: domain, content, code, hosting. He wants to be able to walk away from any vendor, including YohDev, without losing his site.
3. The site working correctly on mobile — he called out the mobile nav as crowded and broken, and this is visible proof of a problem he can show his wife.

## What they care about less

- Visual redesign in this phase — he explicitly agreed to push the full UX refresh to Milestone 2 because the timeline doesn't allow it. Don't oversell design in the Milestone 1 conversation or it will muddy the scope and raise budget anxiety about paying twice.
- Blog content strategy right now — he knows the ProSites blog content has been harmful, but he isn't asking for new content in Milestone 1. Keep SEO content services clearly scoped for a future conversation.

## Watch-outs

- **His wife is a silent decision-maker who hasn't met us yet.** Everything Victor agrees to on a call could get revisited after he talks to her. Build in checkpoints where she can weigh in before anything is finalized, especially on brand direction. The Friday follow-up where they review examples together is the first real test of alignment.
- **Budget anxiety about paying twice.** He raised the concern directly — what if we do the lift-and-shift and then pay again for a redesign? The Milestone 1 / Milestone 2 framing addresses this, but it needs to be positioned carefully in the quote. Sunk cost framing will trigger resistance. Position Milestone 1 as the cost of ownership and freedom, not the cost of delay.
- **ProSites content mess is worse than it looks.** Approximately one blog post per week since 2025 exists on the current site, much of it irrelevant or actively harmful to the practice's positioning. A one-for-one migration will carry that content over. Make sure the client understands that migrating the blog posts is not an endorsement of them — flag clearly what should be reviewed, redirected, or removed before go-live.
- **He is already burned by a vendor relationship.** Any friction in the early engagement — slow responses, vague answers, scope drift — will land harder for him than for a typical new client. Over-communicate and over-document in the first two weeks.

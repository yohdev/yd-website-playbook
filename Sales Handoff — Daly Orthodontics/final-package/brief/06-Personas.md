## Persona 01 — The Local Parent

- **Name placeholder:** Michelle
- **Role / title:** Parent / household healthcare decision-maker
- **Age range:** 32–48
- **Location:** Burlington, NC or nearby (Alamance County)

**What they're trying to do**
> Book an orthodontic consultation for their kid before the school year ends and feel confident they're choosing the right local practice.

**What's in their way**
- The current mobile site is cluttered and hard to navigate on a phone, making it difficult to quickly confirm services, read the doctor's bio, or find a contact number
- Generic dental blog content on the site creates doubt about whether this is actually a specialist orthodontics practice

**Where they find us**
- Google search ("orthodontist Burlington NC", "braces for kids near me") or Google Ads

**What makes them click "Contact"**
- A clear, fast-loading page that immediately confirms the practice does braces and aligners, shows the doctor's face and credentials, and makes calling or booking feel effortless on mobile

**What makes them leave**
- Blog posts about oral surgery or general dentistry that signal "wrong place," a crowded mobile nav that buries the contact button, or a site that looks outdated and untrustworthy next to a competitor

**Direct quote:**
> "I just want to know they do braces for kids, see who the doctor is, and find the phone number without scrolling forever."

## Persona 02 — The Adult Treatment Seeker

- **Name placeholder:** Jordan
- **Role / title:** Working adult considering braces or aligners for themselves
- **Age range:** 22–40
- **Location:** Burlington, NC metro area

**What they're trying to do**
> Quietly research Invisalign or adult braces options at a local orthodontist and get a sense of cost, process, and how approachable the practice feels before committing to a consultation.

**What's in their way**
- Misaligned SEO content (general dentistry, oral surgery posts) makes it unclear whether this practice specializes in the orthodontic treatments they're actually considering
- No confidence-building content — before/after cases, financing info, or a warm doctor introduction — to overcome the hesitation adults feel about starting orthodontic treatment

**Where they find us**
- Google search or Google Ads ("Invisalign Burlington NC," "adult braces near me"), occasionally a social media referral from Maris's content

**What makes them click "Contact"**
- Seeing that the practice treats adults, offers clear aligner options, and has a doctor who feels approachable — ideally with a short, human bio or a welcoming photo of the team

**What makes them leave**
- A site that reads like a generic dental office, blog content about tooth extractions or gum disease that signals "this isn't the right specialist," or a mobile experience so frustrating they give up and Google the next result

**Direct quote:**
> "I've been putting this off for years — I just need to see that they actually do adult Invisalign and that it won't be a weird experience to walk in there as a grown adult."

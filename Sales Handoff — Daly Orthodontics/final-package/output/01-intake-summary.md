## What I've assumed (straw-men for Friday reaction)

These are working hypotheses Andrew should sanity-check before the call. They exist because concrete reactions > abstract adjectives when Dr. Daly is seeing us for the first time.

### Positioning straw-man
> Daly Orthodontics is the specialist orthodontic practice that gives Burlington adults and parents a confident path to braces and aligners — from a locally-owned practice, not a franchise front.

### One-liner straw-man
> "Specialist orthodontic care for Burlington — braces, aligners, and confident smiles, from a practice you actually own a relationship with."

### Voice straw-man
Warm, specialist-confident, locally grounded, plainspoken, never up-sold. Not cutesy/kid-first (serves adults equally), not clinical-cold.

Sample *in voice*:
> "We only do orthodontics — braces and aligners, nothing else — and we've built our practice in Burlington to do that one thing well."

Sample *NOT in voice* (the Pearl Sites style to leave behind):
> "Whether you need braces, extractions, implants, or oral surgery, our dental family is here for all your smile needs."

### Personality dials (hypothesized; ask Dr. Daly to rate 1–5)
| Axis | Hypothesis |
|---|---|
| Serious ↔ Playful | 2 — serious, with warmth |
| Classic ↔ Modern | 3 — balanced (logo is classic, UI should feel contemporary) |
| Quiet ↔ Loud | 2 — restrained |
| Premium ↔ Accessible | 3 — premium feel, accessible tone |
| Literal ↔ Abstract | 2 — concrete |

### Reference directions (three tangible buckets to pick among)
Rather than asking "what do you like?", show three curated buckets and let them eliminate two:
1. **Restrained modern medicine** — e.g., premium dermatology or optometry practice sites that look like brands, not medical clearinghouses.
2. **Boutique hospitality** — warm, serif-forward, photography-led. Closer to a small luxury hotel than a clinic. Honors the gold-on-green's dress-up feel.
3. **Contemporary direct-to-consumer health** — Warby / Hims-style clean layouts, heavy whitespace, sans-led. Trades the logo's classicism for an approachable voice.

My vote before the call: **1 with a dash of 2** — the logo's art-deco serif is already anchoring them toward restrained modern. Going full #3 would make the logo feel like a grandparent at a kids' party.

### Typography direction (Phase 03 proposal, not locked)
- **Headline:** Fraunces (or DM Serif Display / Playfair Display as alternates) — high-contrast serif with character, echoes the logo's deco-serif without copying it.
- **Body:** Inter (or Manrope / Source Sans 3) — humanist sans, renders cleanly at 16–18px, WordPress-safe via Google Fonts.
- **Principle:** no third paid typeface. Self-hosted open-source only, for WP portability and zero ongoing cost.

## Risks / conflicts surfaced

1. **Domain spelling ("daily" vs. "Daly")** is a live inconsistency between the brand mark and the URL. Any fix creates 301 work and possible short-term ranking disruption — do not promise a clean fix inside M1's timeline without digging in first.
2. **Double-pay anxiety (explicit in 08-Client-Profile).** Victor has already surfaced this. The M1 → M2 framing has to be positioned as "pay for ownership, then pay for brand" — not "pay now, pay again later."
3. **Patient Hub migration unknown.** It may be a proprietary Pearl Sites feature. If it doesn't port to WordPress, we need a decision at Friday: replace it, drop it, or delay M1 go-live. This is the single biggest technical risk to the June deadline.
4. **Mustard yellow on the current site ≠ the logo gold.** The current brand expression is using a "close but wrong" accent that dulls the logo. Worth naming out loud on the call so the redesign isn't perceived as changing the color — it's restoring the correct one.
5. **Dr. Daly seeing us for the first time.** Everything in this doc should survive her reading it with Victor on a Thursday evening before the Friday call. Keep language specific, not salesy.

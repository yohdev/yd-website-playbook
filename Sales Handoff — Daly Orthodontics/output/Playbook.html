Section 03

The ProSites Problem — Why You Need to Leave.

Your website isn't yours. The data shows 127 technical SEO errors, mobile performance score of 34/100, and blog content actively discouraging orthodontic patients. Every month you stay costs more money and lost patients.

Migrating without refreshing is half a project.

A pure lift-and-shift puts the same site on a new platform. You'd own it, which solves the ownership problem — but the same mustard-yellow accents, the same stock hero image, the same mobile nav full of items, and the same generic blog content would all come with it. You'd be paying for infrastructure work without fixing the things that are actually costing you patients.

Refreshing without migrating is impossible before June.

ProSites doesn't let you redesign inside their platform at the level we're proposing. A refresh requires the move. And the move requires the refresh to pay for itself.

Doing them separately is the expensive option.

Two projects means two kickoffs, two discovery rounds, two setup fees, two rounds of review-cycle overhead, and — most importantly — a sequential timeline that puts the refresh somewhere in late summer. Bundling them means one price, one calendar, one project manager, one deadline.

What this avoids

Paying twice. On the discovery call you raised a concern about doing a migration now and then paying for a redesign later. This plan is the answer: you pay once, you get both, and you get them before the ProSites billing cycle you're trying to escape.

Section 04

How we'll work.

Six phases, each with a tangible deliverable and an approval gate. You never wonder what we're working on. You see work in progress at every review, not just finished work.

01

Intake

Lock the remaining decisions from the Friday call, confirm scope, baseline the SEO rankings, snapshot every page on the current site.

02

Research

Full crawl of the existing site. Blog triage. Competitor scan. Audit of what to migrate, redirect, or retire.

03

System

Finalize the design system — colors, typography, components. You sign off on the style guide before any page gets built.

04

Build

Homepage, treatment pages, new-patient flow, contact, blog template. Native WordPress blocks. Mobile-first.

05

QA & SEO

Page-by-page comparison against the baseline. Redirect testing. Form testing. Analytics and tracking verified. Accessibility pass.

06

Launch

DNS flip to the new site. ProSites cancellation the same day. Post-launch ranking verification over the following two weeks.

On approvals

We don't move to the next phase without your sign-off on the current one. That's not a formality — it's how we keep the timeline honest. Catching a direction change at Phase 3 costs a day; catching it at Phase 5 costs a week we don't have.
